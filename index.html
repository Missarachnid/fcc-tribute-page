Johannes Kepler

1571-1630

Astronomer, Mathematician and Science Fiction Author

Johannes Kepler was born in 1571 in Weil der Stadt, a city that still stands in south western Germany.
Kepler is best known for his laws of planetary motion.
This information about his life may surprise you!

  • He used Tycho Brahe's observations to create his laws of planetary motion.
  • He had to defend his mother, who was charged with being a witch in 1617.
  • He wrote a manuscript called "Somnium" (Dream), that was a work of science fiction. It wasn't published until after his death.
  • He wrote about the supernova of 1604 in his work "De Stella Nova". It is now called Kepler's Supernova.
  • Due to religious conflict in the region he had to move several times. There are monuments to him in several cities where he worked.
  • The French military did not attack Weil der Stadt during WWII because it was Kepler's birthplace.
  • There is a crater on the moon named after him.
  • The house he was born in still exists in his hometown and now houses a museum.
